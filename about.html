About Us

Legal Basis & History

Citizenship Advancement Training (CAT) is a restructuring of the "Citizen Army Training" and is a component of MAPEH. It is grounded in the 1987 Constitution (Article II, Sec. 13), which recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building and promotes their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being.

According to the National Defense Act (Article I, Sec. 2), the preservation of the State is the obligation of every citizen. CAT aims to inculcate patriotism and nationalism while encouraging involvement in public and civic affairs among students.

This training is a requirement for graduation in Junior High School. Student performance is rated either PASSED or FAILED.
